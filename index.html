About the Temple

A Sacred Abode of Lord Shiva

Sri Mahalingeshwara Temple at Padubelman is a revered Shaivite shrine in coastal Karnataka, dedicated to Lord Mahalingeshwara — a form of Lord Shiva. The temple is cherished by devotees from across the region for its sanctity, ancient traditions, and the deep sense of peace it offers.

The temple follows the Tulu Nadu tradition of worship, with daily rituals conducted by trained priests according to agamic guidelines. It is also home to Lord Ganapathi, whose blessings are invoked at the commencement of all religious activities.

Each year, the temple hosts vibrant festivals including the Brahmakalasha, Kola, and the Utsava — events that draw thousands of devotees and echo with the sound of devotional music, chants, and ancient ceremonies.

Daily Observances

Sacred Rituals & Timings

Each day at the temple follows a rhythmic cycle of worship, offering devotees multiple opportunities to receive divine blessings

Morning Pooja

7:00 AM – 8:00 AM
The day begins with the ceremonial awakening of the deity — Mangalarati — followed by abhisheka (sacred bath) with water and panchamruta. The priest adorns the Linga with fresh flowers and performs Rudrabhisheka with Sri Rudram chanting. Naivedya (food offering) is presented and arati concludes the morning session.

Evening Pooja

5:00 PM – 7:30 PM
As dusk falls, the temple comes alive with the glow of lamps. The evening pooja begins with deepa aradhana (lamp worship) and is accompanied by devotional music. The Shiva Linga is decorated with new flowers for the evening session and the Mangalarati brings the day's worship to a serene and luminous close.

Ranga Pooje

6:00 PM (On Request)
A special evening ritual performed only upon devotee request. Freshly cooked rice is first offered to both Lord Mahalingeshwara and Lord Ganapathi. Following this, the Bali ritual is performed — uncooked rice placed on leaves is offered at Bali stones inside and outside the temple with deep reverence.

Shatarudrabhisheka

Special Occasions
One of the most powerful sevas at the temple — Sri Rudram is chanted one hundred times while the Linga is bathed with holy water, panchamruta, tender coconut, and other sacred offerings. This is typically performed during important festivals, milestones, or by devotees seeking profound spiritual transformation.
